Audio Visualisation

This is a Unity Project where sound is visualized by movement and a nice effect created by a shader.

The visuals move horizontally based on the most prominent frequency, and vertically based on the amplitude. When clicking the "Show Info" button, you can see that the next to the frequency and the amplitude, the closest tone is also calculated. Each frame, the measured frequency is compared to a list containing all 88 piano notes in a C# script, and the closest tone is displayed.

The visuals are created using Unity's trail renderer and Shadergraph. The shader is based on the underwater world shader from Peer Play's YouTube video series "Underwater World Shaders".

This was my first time using ShaderGraph, the trail renderer and doing frequency-related audio stuff, so it was a very fun and educational experience to create this.
